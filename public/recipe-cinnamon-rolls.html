Homemade Cinnamon Rolls

⏱ Prep: 30 min🔥 Cook: 25 min⏳ Total: 2.5 hrs (incl. rising)🍽 Makes: 12 rolls
380Calories
6gProtein
16gFat
54gCarbs

There are very few aromas in this world that can rival the intoxicating scent of freshly baked cinnamon rolls wafting through your kitchen on a weekend morning. These homemade cinnamon rolls are the ultimate expression of comfort baking — pillowy soft, enriched dough that has been lovingly rolled out, generously smeared with a thick layer of cinnamon and brown sugar butter, then tightly spiraled, sliced, and baked until golden and bubbling. The final crowning touch is a lavish drizzle of tangy cream cheese icing that melts into every warm crevice of the freshly baked rolls, creating an experience that is nothing short of transcendent.

Making cinnamon rolls from scratch is a deeply rewarding process that fills your entire home with the most heavenly smell imaginable. While the recipe does require some patience for the dough to rise properly, the actual hands-on work is surprisingly straightforward and therapeutic. There is something profoundly satisfying about kneading soft, warm dough with your hands, watching it double in size like magic, and rolling it out into a perfect rectangle before adding that decadent cinnamon-sugar filling. The result is a batch of cinnamon rolls that are infinitely better than anything you could ever buy from a store or chain bakery.

The secret to achieving the softest, most cloud-like texture lies in the enriched dough, which uses a combination of butter, milk, eggs, and just the right amount of flour to create a dough that is rich and tender without being heavy or dense. The warm milk activates the yeast perfectly, the butter adds richness and a soft crumb, and the eggs provide structure and a gorgeous golden color when baked. Combined with a generous cinnamon-brown sugar filling and that signature cream cheese frosting, these rolls are genuinely life-changing.

Dough Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm milk (110°F / 43°C — warm to touch, not hot)
  • 2.25 teaspoons active dry yeast (one standard packet)
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)

Cinnamon Filling Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 3/4 cup packed dark brown sugar
  • 2.5 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Cream Cheese Icing

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1.5 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons milk

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast: Pour the warm milk into a large mixing bowl and sprinkle the yeast and a pinch of the sugar over the surface. Let it sit undisturbed for five to eight minutes until the mixture becomes frothy and bubbly on top, which confirms the yeast is alive and active. If it does not foam, your milk was likely too hot or too cold and you should start over with fresh yeast and properly warmed milk.
  2. Make the dough: Add the remaining sugar, melted butter, eggs, and salt to the yeast mixture. Whisk until well combined. Begin adding the flour one cup at a time, stirring with a wooden spoon after each addition. Once the dough becomes too thick to stir, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead by hand for eight to ten minutes until the dough is smooth, soft, elastic, and springs back when you poke it gently with a finger. It should feel slightly tacky but not stick to your hands. Place the dough in a large, lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and set it in a warm, draft-free spot to rise for one hour, or until it has roughly doubled in size.
  3. Prepare the filling: While the dough rises, combine the softened butter, dark brown sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Mix until it forms a thick, spreadable paste that smells absolutely incredible.
  4. Roll and fill: Once the dough has doubled, punch it down gently to release the air and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll it into a large rectangle, approximately sixteen inches by twelve inches. Spread the cinnamon-brown sugar filling evenly across the entire surface of the dough, leaving a half-inch border along one long edge. Press the filling gently into the dough so it stays in place during rolling.
  5. Roll and slice: Starting from the long edge opposite the border, roll the dough tightly into a log, keeping it as even and snug as possible. Pinch the seam to seal it. Using a sharp knife or unflavored dental floss, cut the log into twelve equal rolls. Place them cut-side up in a greased nine-by-thirteen-inch baking dish, spacing them about half an inch apart. Cover with plastic wrap and let them rise for another thirty to forty-five minutes until they have puffed up noticeably and the rolls are nearly touching each other.
  6. Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove the plastic wrap and bake the rolls for twenty-two to twenty-six minutes, until the tops are a light golden brown. They should feel set but still soft when you press the top gently. Do not overbake — the rolls will continue cooking slightly from residual heat as they cool, and slightly underbaking is the secret to the softest, gooiest cinnamon rolls.
  7. Make the icing and serve: While the rolls bake, beat together the softened cream cheese and butter until smooth and fluffy. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla, and milk, beating until you have a thick but pourable icing. As soon as the rolls come out of the oven, while they are still piping hot, drizzle the cream cheese icing generously over the entire batch. The heat of the rolls will melt the icing perfectly so it settles into every warm spiral and crevice. Serve immediately for the ultimate indulgent breakfast experience.
🍯 Savory Spoon Tips:
• For overnight cinnamon rolls, complete the recipe through the slicing step, place in the baking dish, cover tightly with plastic wrap, and refrigerate overnight. In the morning, remove from the fridge, let them sit at room temperature for thirty minutes while the oven preheats, then bake as directed. Freshly baked cinnamon rolls for weekend brunch with zero morning effort.
• Dark brown sugar produces a richer, more caramel-like filling than light brown sugar, but either will work beautifully.
• The dental floss slicing trick creates the cleanest, most even cuts — slide a piece of floss under the log, cross it over the top, and pull to slice through.
• These rolls freeze beautifully after baking and icing. Wrap individually and reheat in the microwave for twenty seconds for a quick treat anytime.
