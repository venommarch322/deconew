Roasted Pumpkin Soup

⏱ Prep: 15 min🔥 Cook: 40 min⏳ Total: 55 min🍽 Serves: 6
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

There is an almost magical transformation that happens when pumpkin meets high heat in the oven. Raw pumpkin is dense, watery, and relatively bland — but when it is cut into wedges, drizzled with olive oil, and roasted at a high temperature until the edges caramelize into deep golden-brown patches, it becomes something entirely different: intensely sweet, deeply flavored, silky-textured, and extraordinarily aromatic. This roasted pumpkin soup harnesses that transformation to create what is arguably the most satisfying, warming, and delicious soup you can make during the autumn and winter months. Every spoonful tastes like a concentrated essence of fall — warm, subtly sweet, gently spiced, and incredibly comforting.

The foundation of this soup is the roasting technique itself. By roasting the pumpkin along with onions, garlic, and a blend of warm spices before blending everything into a soup, you build layers of caramelized flavor that are simply impossible to achieve by boiling or sautéing pumpkin on the stovetop. The Maillard reaction that occurs during roasting creates hundreds of complex flavor compounds that add depth, sweetness, and a subtle nuttiness to the soup. A splash of coconut cream stirred in at the end adds luxurious richness and a hint of tropical sweetness that complements the earthy pumpkin beautifully, while toasted pepitas scattered over the top provide a satisfying crunch that contrasts with the velvety smooth texture underneath.

This soup is naturally gluten-free, dairy-free (when using coconut cream), and packed with vitamins A, C, and fiber from the pumpkin — making it not only delicious but genuinely nutritious. It is the kind of recipe that makes you feel like you are nourishing both body and soul simultaneously, and it is simple enough for a casual weeknight dinner yet elegant enough to serve as a first course at a dinner party. The gorgeous, vibrant orange color looks absolutely stunning in white bowls, and the garnish of cream swirl and toasted pepitas adds a professional, restaurant-quality visual touch that belies how easy the recipe truly is.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium sugar pumpkin (about 3 lbs) — halved, seeded, and cut into wedges (or 2 cans of pure pumpkin puree)
  • 1 large yellow onion, quartered
  • 6 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream (or heavy cream)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper
  • Toasted pepitas and a swirl of cream for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Roast the pumpkin: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Arrange the pumpkin wedges cut-side up on a large, rimmed baking sheet along with the quartered onion and unpeeled garlic cloves. Drizzle generously with olive oil and sprinkle with the cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to coat everything evenly. Roast for thirty-five to forty minutes until the pumpkin flesh is completely tender when pierced with a fork and the edges have caramelized into beautiful golden-brown patches. The onions should be soft and lightly charred, and the garlic should be squeezably soft inside its papery skin. This roasting step is where all the magic happens — it concentrates the pumpkin's natural sugars and builds the deep, complex flavor foundation of the entire soup.
  2. Scoop and blend: Remove from the oven and let cool for ten minutes. Scoop the soft pumpkin flesh from the skin and place it into a large pot. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves from their skins into the same pot. Add the roasted onion pieces and the vegetable broth. Using an immersion blender directly in the pot, blend on high speed for two to three minutes until the soup is completely smooth, velvety, and uniform in color with no chunks remaining whatsoever. Alternatively, carefully blend in batches in a standing blender, filling only halfway each time.
  3. Finish the soup: Place the pot over medium-low heat and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the coconut cream and maple syrup. The cream adds body and richness while the maple syrup enhances the pumpkin's natural sweetness and adds a subtle caramel note. Taste and adjust seasoning — you may want more salt, a pinch more cinnamon, or an extra drizzle of maple syrup depending on the sweetness of your particular pumpkin. Simmer for five more minutes to let the flavors come together.
  4. Serve: Ladle into warm bowls. Drizzle a thin spiral of coconut cream or regular cream over the top. Scatter a generous handful of toasted pepitas over each serving for crunch. Finish with a crack of black pepper and a tiny pinch of flaky sea salt. Serve alongside warm sourdough bread or crusty rolls for dipping.
🫒 Olive & Thyme Tips:
• Sugar pumpkins (also called pie pumpkins) have sweeter, denser flesh than large carving pumpkins. If you cannot find them, butternut squash is an excellent substitute that produces equally delicious results.
• For maximum convenience, use two cans of pure pumpkin puree instead of roasting fresh pumpkin — simply sauté the onion and garlic on the stovetop, add the puree and broth, and proceed as directed.
• A tablespoon of miso paste stirred into the soup adds incredible umami depth without changing the flavor profile noticeably.
• This soup freezes beautifully for up to four months. Portion into freezer containers for instant warm meals on cold days.
• Toast pepitas in a dry skillet over medium heat for three to four minutes, tossing frequently, until they puff up and become golden and fragrant.
