Chicken Shawarma

⏱ Prep: 15 min🔥 Cook: 25 min⏳ Total: 45 min🍽 Serves: 4
440Calories
38gProtein
22gFat
24gCarbs

Chicken shawarma is street food royalty across the Middle East — thin layers of warmly spiced chicken stacked on a vertical rotisserie, slowly roasting until the edges become crispy and caramelized while the interior stays impossibly juicy and tender. The aroma of shawarma sizzling on a street-corner spit is one of the most irresistible and universally appetizing smells in the culinary world, drawing in passersby from blocks away with its intoxicating blend of cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, and garlic mingling with the rich, savory scent of roasting chicken. While most of us do not have a vertical rotisserie in our kitchens, this oven-roasted version recreates the authentic flavor profile with stunning accuracy and is designed specifically for weeknight home cooking.

The secret to achieving that distinctive shawarma flavor at home lies in the marinade — a carefully balanced blend of warm spices, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and yogurt that tenderizes the chicken while infusing it with layers of complex, aromatic flavor. The spice blend is what distinguishes shawarma from other grilled chicken preparations — it is warm and earthy rather than fiery hot, with cumin providing its characteristic dusty warmth, coriander adding a citrusy brightness, turmeric contributing golden color and subtle bitterness, smoked paprika lending a touch of smokiness, and a whisper of cinnamon adding an unexpected but essential sweetness that rounds out the entire profile into something extraordinary and distinctly Middle Eastern.

The chicken is roasted at high heat on a sheet pan, which allows the edges to become beautifully caramelized and slightly charred — mimicking the crispy exterior of rotisserie-style shawarma — while the inside stays succulent and tender. Served in warm pita or flatbread with a generous drizzle of creamy garlic sauce, crunchy pickled red onions, fresh herbs, and crisp vegetables, this chicken shawarma is a complete meal that is simultaneously healthy, deeply satisfying, and bursting with bold, authentic Middle Eastern flavors that will transport you to the bustling streets of Beirut or Istanbul.

Shawarma Spice Marinade

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper

Garlic Sauce (Toum)

  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

To Serve

  • Warm pita bread or flatbread
  • Pickled red onions
  • Fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce
  • Fresh parsley and mint
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients thoroughly. Add the chicken thighs and toss until every piece is completely coated in the vibrant, spiced mixture. Cover and refrigerate for a minimum of one hour — overnight is ideal and produces dramatically more flavorful, tender results. The yogurt in the marinade acts as a natural tenderizer, breaking down protein fibers while the lemon juice brightens the spices and the garlic infuses deep, pungent flavor into the meat. If you are short on time, even thirty minutes at room temperature will produce noticeable results.
  2. Roast at high heat: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a large sheet pan with parchment paper. Arrange the marinated chicken thighs in a single layer, making sure none overlap. Roast for twenty to twenty-five minutes until the chicken is cooked through with an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) and the edges are beautifully charred and caramelized. For extra char, switch the oven to broil for the final two to three minutes, watching carefully to prevent burning. The high heat creates that signature shawarma texture — slightly crispy and caramelized on the outside, juicy and tender on the inside.
  3. Slice the chicken: Remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for five minutes. Using a sharp knife, slice each thigh into thin strips against the grain. This cutting technique ensures every bite is tender and easy to chew, and the thin strips are perfect for tucking into pita bread or piling on a plate.
  4. Make the garlic sauce: While the chicken roasts, stir together the Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, grated garlic, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Taste and adjust — you want it tangy, garlicky, and creamy. This sauce can be made up to three days ahead and stored in the refrigerator.
  5. Assemble and serve: Warm the pita bread in the oven for one minute or over a gas flame for thirty seconds per side. Spread a generous layer of garlic sauce inside each pita. Fill with the sliced shawarma chicken, pickled red onions, sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, fresh herbs, and a drizzle of hot sauce if desired. Roll tightly and serve immediately, or plate the components separately for a build-your-own shawarma dinner spread that is incredibly fun and interactive.
🫒 Olive & Thyme Tips:
• Chicken thighs are non-negotiable — they stay far juicier and more flavorful than breasts during high-heat roasting.
• Quick-pickle red onions by soaking thinly sliced onions in a mixture of equal parts vinegar and water with a pinch of sugar and salt for twenty minutes. They add a crucial tangy crunch.
• For an even more authentic experience, let the cooked, sliced chicken rest in a covered pan for five minutes — the residual steam softens the chicken and makes it even more succulent.
• This shawarma chicken also works beautifully over rice bowls, in salads, or stuffed into wraps with hummus.
• Double the spice blend recipe and store it in a jar — you will have instant shawarma seasoning ready whenever you want.
