Classic Caesar Salad

⏱ Prep: 15 min🔥 Cook: 5 min⏳ Total: 20 min🍽 Serves: 4
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

The Caesar salad is perhaps the most iconic salad in the entire Western culinary canon, and for generations it has maintained its place on restaurant menus and dinner tables around the world because when it is made properly — with a rich, punchy, garlicky dressing made from scratch, the crispest hearts of romaine lettuce, freshly shaved Parmesan cheese, and crunchy golden homemade croutons — it is simply unbeatable. This is not a boring side salad filled with limp lettuce and bottled dressing from a squeeze bottle. This is a bold, flavorful, deeply savory dish that can absolutely stand on its own as a satisfying meal or serve as the most impressive starter to any dinner party.

The legend of the Caesar salad traces back to 1924, when Italian-American restaurateur Caesar Cardini supposedly invented it on the fly at his restaurant in Tijuana, Mexico, using whatever ingredients he had on hand during a busy Fourth of July rush. Whether or not the origin story is entirely accurate, what remains undeniably true is that the combination of flavors and textures in a Caesar salad is pure genius — the cool, crunchy snap of romaine, the rich and savory umami depth of the dressing with its anchovy backbone and sharp Parmesan bite, the satisfying crunch of garlicky croutons, and the bright, cutting acidity of fresh lemon juice that ties everything together in perfect harmony.

The homemade dressing is what elevates this salad from ordinary to extraordinary, and it takes only five minutes to make. Once you taste a Caesar salad dressed with a freshly made dressing — thick, creamy, gloriously garlicky, with that beautiful balance of savory, tangy, and slightly salty — you will never go back to the bottled stuff again. The dressing emulsifies beautifully when you add the olive oil in a slow, steady stream while whisking vigorously, creating a consistency that clings to every leaf of romaine and coats each crouton in rich, punchy flavor.

Dressing Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves, minced to a paste
  • 2 anchovy fillets, finely minced (or 1 tsp anchovy paste)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper

Salad Ingredients

  • 2 large heads romaine lettuce, washed and chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • Parmesan shavings for topping
  • Homemade croutons: 3 cups cubed day-old bread, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 garlic clove minced, salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make croutons: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss the cubed bread with olive oil, minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for ten to twelve minutes, tossing halfway through, until golden brown and crunchy on all sides. These homemade croutons are incomparably better than store-bought — they are crisp, garlicky, and have a satisfying crunch that holds up beautifully against the dressing without turning soggy.
  2. Make the dressing: In a medium bowl, combine the minced garlic paste and anchovy fillets. Use the back of a fork to mash them together into a smooth paste — this creates the umami-rich foundation that gives Caesar dressing its distinctive savory depth. Add the lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and egg yolk. Whisk vigorously until well combined and slightly frothy. While whisking constantly, begin adding the olive oil in a very slow, thin, steady stream. This gradual addition is essential for creating a proper emulsion — the dressing will thicken and become creamy as the oil incorporates. Once all the oil is added, stir in the grated Parmesan and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Assemble the salad: Place the chopped romaine lettuce in a very large serving bowl. Pour the dressing over the lettuce — start with about three-quarters of the dressing and add more to your preference. Toss vigorously with tongs or clean hands, making sure every leaf is evenly and generously coated. Add the homemade croutons and toss gently once more.
  4. Serve: Divide the dressed salad among chilled plates. Top each serving with generous Parmesan shavings — use a vegetable peeler to create beautiful, wide curls directly from a block of Parmesan. Finish with a generous crack of fresh black pepper and serve immediately while the lettuce is still cold and crisp and the croutons are still perfectly crunchy.
🍯 Savory Spoon Tips:
• If using raw egg yolk concerns you, substitute with two tablespoons of high-quality mayonnaise for a similar creamy texture without the raw egg.
• Do not skip the anchovies — they do not make the dressing taste fishy at all. Instead, they provide a deep, savory umami backbone that is completely irreplaceable. You will not taste fish, just incredible depth of flavor.
• Always use romaine hearts — they are crisper, sweeter, and have a better crunch than the outer leaves.
• For a complete meal, top with grilled chicken breast sliced on the bias, seared shrimp, or a soft six-minute egg.
• Make the dressing up to three days ahead and store in the refrigerator. The flavors actually improve as they meld together.
