Chicken Tikka Masala

⏱ Prep: 20 min 🔥 Cook: 30 min ⏳ Total: 50 min 🍽 Serves: 4
495Calories
36gProtein
28gFat
22gCarbs
[ Google Ad — In-Article Responsive ]

Chicken tikka masala holds a legendary status in the world of global cuisine, and for very good reason. This iconic dish features tender chunks of chicken that have been marinated in a fragrant blend of yogurt and warm spices, then cooked until slightly charred and smoky, before being enveloped in one of the most luxuriously creamy, richly spiced tomato sauces you will ever taste. Every spoonful delivers a complex harmony of flavors — the smoky depth of the chicken, the bright acidity of the tomatoes, the gentle warmth of garam masala and smoked paprika, and the silky richness of cream that ties everything together into pure, irresistible comfort.

What makes this recipe special is the attention to building layers of flavor at every stage of the cooking process. The yogurt marinade does far more than just add taste — its natural acidity breaks down the proteins in the chicken, tenderizing the meat from the inside out so that every piece is succulent and melt-in-your-mouth soft. The spices in the marinade penetrate deeply during the resting period, ensuring the chicken is flavorful through and through, not just on the surface. When the marinated chicken is seared at high heat, the sugars in the yogurt caramelize and create those beautiful charred edges that give tikka its signature smoky character.

The sauce itself is a masterclass in simplicity and depth. Onions, garlic, and ginger are cooked slowly until they melt into a soft, aromatic base. Crushed tomatoes are added and simmered until they transform from sharp and tangy to mellow and richly concentrated. Heavy cream is stirred in to create that iconic warm, sunset-orange color and velvety texture, while a touch of honey rounds out any remaining acidity and adds a subtle sweetness that makes the dish absolutely addictive. The result is a sauce so delicious you will want to eat it by the spoonful.

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs — cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain full-fat yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons tikka masala paste or curry paste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt to taste

For the masala sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, very finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine the cubed chicken thighs with the yogurt, tikka paste, smoked paprika, cumin, turmeric, lemon juice, and a generous pinch of salt. Mix thoroughly until every piece of chicken is completely coated in the vibrant, spiced marinade. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and place it in the refrigerator for a minimum of one hour — though marinating for four hours or even overnight will yield dramatically more flavorful and tender results. The yogurt works as a natural tenderizer, breaking down the protein fibers in the chicken while the spices infuse every layer of the meat with extraordinary aromatic depth. This is the most important step in the entire recipe, so do not rush it.
  2. Cook the chicken: When you are ready to cook, heat a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken pieces from the marinade, shaking off any excess but leaving a thin coating on each piece. Working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan, sear the chicken for three to four minutes per side until each piece develops a gorgeous, slightly charred, golden-brown exterior with beautiful caramelized edges. The chicken does not need to be completely cooked through at this stage because it will finish cooking in the sauce. Those charred edges are what give tikka its unmistakable smoky flavor that you simply cannot replicate any other way. Transfer the seared chicken to a clean plate and set it aside.
  3. Build the sauce: In the same pan, lower the heat to medium and add the butter. Once it melts and begins to foam, add the finely diced onion. Cook for six to seven minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is completely soft, translucent, and just beginning to turn golden at the edges. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for another sixty seconds, stirring constantly, until the kitchen fills with an incredibly fragrant aroma that announces something wonderful is about to happen.
  4. Add tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes, along with the garam masala and smoked paprika. Stir everything together thoroughly, making sure to scrape up any browned bits stuck to the bottom of the pan — those caramelized remnants from the chicken contain concentrated flavor that will enrich the sauce enormously. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook undisturbed for eight to ten minutes, stirring occasionally, until the raw tomato flavor mellows and the sauce thickens noticeably into a rich, concentrated base.
  5. Cream and simmer: Reduce the heat to low and pour in the heavy cream, stirring gently until the sauce transforms into a gorgeous, warm sunset-orange color with a velvety, luxurious consistency. Stir in the honey — this small addition makes a tremendous difference, balancing the acidity of the tomatoes and adding a subtle sweetness that makes the dish irresistibly more-ish. Taste the sauce at this point and adjust the salt and pepper to your personal preference. Return the seared chicken pieces to the pan, nestling them into the creamy sauce. Let everything simmer together on low heat for twelve to fifteen minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is completely cooked through and has absorbed the incredible flavors of the masala sauce.
  6. Serve: Transfer to a warm serving dish and garnish generously with freshly torn cilantro leaves. Serve immediately alongside fluffy steamed basmati rice, warm garlic naan bread, or both for the most satisfying and authentic experience. A simple side of cucumber raita — yogurt mixed with grated cucumber, a pinch of cumin, and fresh mint — provides a cool, refreshing contrast that perfectly complements the rich, warming curry.
🍯 Savory Spoon Tips:
• Chicken thighs are strongly recommended over breasts — they remain juicier and more tender during the cooking process because of their higher fat content.
• If you cannot find prepared tikka masala paste at your grocery store, you can substitute it with two teaspoons of garam masala, one teaspoon of cumin, one teaspoon of coriander, and half a teaspoon of chili powder mixed together.
• For an incredible smoky depth without a grill, char the marinated chicken under your oven broiler on high for four to five minutes per side instead of pan-searing.
• This dish improves dramatically when made ahead — the flavors continue to develop and deepen as the sauce rests overnight in the refrigerator. Simply reheat gently on the stovetop the next day.
• Freeze portions in airtight containers for up to three months for quick, restaurant-quality dinners on busy weeknights.
