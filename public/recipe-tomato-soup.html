Roasted Tomato Soup

⏱ Prep: 10 min🔥 Cook: 40 min⏳ Total: 50 min🍽 Serves: 6
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

Roasted tomato soup is comfort food in its purest, most elemental form — a bowl of silky, deeply flavored warmth that wraps around you like a blanket on a cold, rainy day. This recipe transforms ordinary canned tomatoes (or fresh summer tomatoes when they are in season) into something truly extraordinary through the magic of roasting. When tomatoes are roasted at high heat in the oven, their natural sugars caramelize and concentrate, their acidity mellows and softens, and their flavor deepens exponentially into a rich, almost sweet, intensely tomatoey essence that forms the perfect foundation for an unforgettable soup.

Unlike thin, watery tomato soups from a can that taste metallic and one-dimensional, this homemade version has an incredible depth of flavor that comes from layering roasted tomatoes with caramelized garlic, sweet onions, aromatic fresh basil, and a splash of cream that adds luxurious body and a beautiful pale orange color. The roasted garlic becomes soft and jammy in the oven, losing all of its sharp raw bite and transforming into a sweet, mellow, deeply nutty paste that dissolves into the soup and enriches every spoonful with subtle complexity.

After blending, the soup becomes velvety smooth — the kind of consistency where the spoon glides through effortlessly and the soup coats the back of it in a thin, even layer. It is the quintessential partner for a golden, buttery grilled cheese sandwich, creating one of the most iconic and universally loved food pairings in existence. There is genuinely no better lunch on a chilly afternoon than dunking a crispy, cheese-oozing grilled cheese into a bowl of this magnificent roasted tomato soup and letting the flavors merge together in warm, comforting perfection.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (28 oz each) whole peeled San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 large yellow onion, quartered
  • 8 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (or honey)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper
  • Crusty bread or grilled cheese for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Roast tomatoes and garlic: Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Pour the canned tomatoes with their juices onto a large, rimmed baking sheet. Add the quartered onion and unpeeled garlic cloves. Drizzle everything generously with olive oil and sprinkle with the oregano, sugar, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to coat. Spread in an even layer and roast for thirty to thirty-five minutes until the tomatoes are deeply caramelized with charred edges, the onions are soft and golden, and the garlic cloves are completely soft and squeezable. The caramelization that occurs during roasting is where the magic happens — it transforms the tomatoes from acidic and one-dimensional to sweet, rich, and profoundly flavorful.
  2. Squeeze the garlic: Remove the baking sheet from the oven and let it cool for five minutes. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves out of their papery skins directly onto the roasted tomatoes — they will pop out easily as soft, golden paste. Discard the skins.
  3. Blend the soup: Transfer the entire contents of the baking sheet — tomatoes, onions, garlic, juices, and all — into a large pot. Add the broth and fresh basil leaves. Using an immersion blender directly in the pot, blend until completely smooth and velvety. Alternatively, carefully transfer in batches to a standing blender, filling only halfway each time and holding the lid down firmly with a towel to prevent hot soup from erupting. Blend until silky smooth with absolutely no chunks remaining.
  4. Finish the soup: Place the pot over medium-low heat and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the heavy cream until the soup takes on a beautiful, warm orange-pink hue and the texture becomes even more luxuriously smooth and velvety. Taste carefully and adjust the seasoning — add more salt for depth, more sugar if the tomatoes are still too acidic, or a crack of black pepper for gentle warmth. Simmer for five more minutes to let everything come together harmoniously.
  5. Serve: Ladle the soup into warm bowls. Finish each serving with a swirl of extra cream, a drizzle of your best olive oil, a few torn fresh basil leaves, and a generous crack of black pepper. Serve immediately alongside thick slices of crusty bread for dipping or, for the ultimate experience, a golden, buttery grilled cheese sandwich made with sharp cheddar on sourdough bread.
🍯 Savory Spoon Tips:
• San Marzano tomatoes are the gold standard for tomato soup — they are sweeter, less acidic, and have a more concentrated tomato flavor than regular canned tomatoes.
• Roasting the garlic in its skin keeps it from burning and produces a sweet, mellow flavor completely different from raw garlic.
• For a vegan version, simply omit the cream or substitute with coconut cream for a different but equally delicious result.
• This soup freezes beautifully for up to four months — make a double batch and portion into freezer-safe containers for quick, warming meals all winter long.
• Add a tablespoon of balsamic vinegar just before serving for a sophisticated tang that brightens all the flavors.
