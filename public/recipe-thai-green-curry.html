Thai Green Curry

⏱ Prep: 10 min🔥 Cook: 25 min⏳ Total: 35 min🍽 Serves: 4
420Calories
30gProtein
26gFat
18gCarbs

Thai green curry is one of the most aromatic and deeply satisfying dishes in all of Southeast Asian cuisine. Known as "kaeng khiao wan" in Thai, which literally translates to "sweet green curry," this dish is a masterful balancing act of flavors — the fiery heat of green chilies tempered by the rich, soothing creaminess of coconut milk, the bright citrus notes of lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves, the savory depth of fish sauce, and the herbal freshness of Thai basil that perfumes each spoonful with an almost floral quality. It is simultaneously complex and comforting, exotic and accessible, spicy and soothing — a dish of beautiful contradictions that somehow works in perfect harmony.

What makes this recipe truly exceptional for the home cook is how remarkably quick and easy it is to prepare, especially when using store-bought green curry paste as a shortcut. With quality curry paste as your foundation, you can have a restaurant-caliber Thai green curry on the table in just thirty-five minutes from start to finish — faster than ordering takeout and infinitely more delicious. The paste does the heavy lifting of providing that complex base of aromatics, while you layer in fresh ingredients and coconut milk to build a curry that is rich, fragrant, and deeply satisfying with every single bite.

This is one of those dishes that tastes incredibly impressive and sophisticated but requires very little actual culinary skill to prepare. If you can stir a pot and chop vegetables, you can make a Thai green curry that will genuinely rival what you would be served at a quality Thai restaurant. The key is using full-fat coconut milk for the richest, most luxurious texture, seasoning boldly with fish sauce and a touch of sugar for that authentic Thai balance of salty-sweet-spicy, and finishing with a generous handful of fresh Thai basil that wilts into the hot curry and releases its intoxicating aroma.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 cans (14 oz each) full-fat coconut milk
  • 3-4 tablespoons Thai green curry paste (adjust for heat preference)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 can (8 oz) bamboo shoots, drained
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 small zucchini, halved and sliced
  • 1 cup Thai eggplant or regular eggplant, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves (optional but highly recommended)
  • Steamed jasmine rice for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Bloom the curry paste: Open one can of coconut milk and scoop the thick cream from the top into a large, deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the green curry paste and stir vigorously for two to three minutes until the paste becomes incredibly fragrant, the oils begin to separate and shimmer on the surface, and you can see tiny bubbles forming. This blooming step is absolutely critical — it activates the essential oils in the aromatics within the paste and creates a flavor foundation that is exponentially more complex than simply dumping the paste into liquid. The kitchen will smell absolutely extraordinary at this point.
  2. Cook the chicken: Add the sliced chicken to the pan and stir to coat every piece in the fragrant curry paste mixture. Cook for four to five minutes, turning the pieces occasionally, until the chicken is sealed on all sides and beginning to take on a pale, opaque appearance. The chicken will finish cooking fully as it simmers in the sauce, so you are not looking for it to be completely done at this stage — just sealed and coated.
  3. Add coconut milk and vegetables: Pour in the remaining coconut milk from both cans and stir until the sauce is smooth, creamy, and a beautiful pale green color. Add the bamboo shoots, red bell pepper, zucchini, eggplant, and kaffir lime leaves if using. Bring the curry to a gentle simmer and cook for twelve to fifteen minutes until the vegetables are tender but still retain some texture and the chicken is completely cooked through. Avoid boiling vigorously as this can cause the coconut milk to separate.
  4. Season: Add the fish sauce and palm sugar, stirring to dissolve the sugar completely. Taste the curry — this is where you achieve that essential Thai balance. It should be simultaneously creamy, spicy, salty, and subtly sweet. Adjust the fish sauce for more saltiness, sugar for more sweetness, or add another spoonful of curry paste if you want more heat. Getting this balance right is what separates a good green curry from an unforgettable one.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove the pan from heat. Tear the fresh Thai basil leaves and stir them into the hot curry — the residual heat will wilt them perfectly and release their magnificent aroma without overcooking them. Ladle the curry generously over steamed jasmine rice in deep bowls. Garnish with a few whole basil leaves on top and serve immediately while the curry is piping hot and the basil is still vibrant and fragrant.
🍯 Savory Spoon Tips:
• Always use full-fat coconut milk — lite versions produce a thin, watery curry that lacks the rich, luxurious body this dish demands.
• Quality curry paste makes a massive difference. Mae Ploy and Maesri are excellent, widely available brands that produce authentic results.
• If you cannot find Thai basil, Italian sweet basil is an acceptable substitute, though the flavor profile will be slightly different. Thai basil has a distinctive anise-like quality that Italian basil lacks.
• Kaffir lime leaves add an irreplaceable citrus perfume — find them in the frozen section of Asian grocery stores.
• For a vegetarian version, substitute the chicken with extra-firm tofu and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce. Add the tofu near the end to maintain its texture.
